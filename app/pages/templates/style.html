Distinctiveness

Distinctiveness is our way of capturing how accurate an artificial intelligence model trained using a single musical detail is in identifing the pianist playing on a recording.

If a pianist has high distinctiveness for harmony, for instance, then they can be recognised using only their chord voicings! Vice versa, if a pianist has low distinctiveness for rhythm, then they are often confused for other pianists when considering their only their time feel.

Overall distinctiveness reflects how accurate our model is when considering all musical details (melody, harmony, rhythm, and dynamics).

Similar Pianists

Similarity is our way of capturing which pianists our model confuses for each other when considering particular musical details. You can change which musical detail is shown by clicking the tabs along the top of the plot.

So, if Keith Jarrett is often confused for Bill Evans when considering their use of harmony, we can say that they are similar to each other along this musical dimension.

For clarity, we only show the top-5 most similar pianists on these plots.
